3. Model Performance & Feature Importance

Methodology:
  • Model: XGBoost gradient-boosted classifier with moderate class weighting (3x)
  • Train/Test Split: Time-based — trained on periods 202404–202509 (262,302 obs, 584 prepays); tested on 202510, 202511, 202512, 202601, 202602, 202603 (89,705 obs, 318 prepays)
  • Features: Refi incentive, penalty points, loan age, remaining term, coupon rate, loan size (log UPB), unit count, penalty status, state, pool type, green/affordable flags
  • Test AUC: 0.722 · Test Brier Score: 0.0036
Top predictors (SHAP): Remaining term and loan age are the strongest predictors — loans nearing maturity or with long seasoning prepay much more often. Loan size (log UPB) and unit count capture borrower sophistication. Refi incentive and penalty points drive the economic motivation layer.

5. Key Findings & Conclusions

  1. Refi incentive is a key economic driver but the S-curve is flatter than in single-family MBS due to commercial borrower heterogeneity, prepayment penalties, and defeasance costs.
  2. Penalty protection is highly effective: Loans with active prepayment penalties show near-zero CPRs regardless of refi incentive. Once restrictions expire, CPR jumps significantly.
  3. Seasoning matters enormously: Remaining term and loan age are the top two predictors. Loans within 5 years of maturity have elevated prepay rates (balloon risk, refinance timing).
  4. Size effect: Larger loans (higher UPB, more units) prepay faster, likely because larger borrowers have more refinancing options and greater financial sophistication.
  5. Geography and program type: Certain states (IN, IL, MN) and FHA programs show distinct prepayment profiles, likely driven by local market conditions and program-specific rules.
  6. Model limitations: With only 902 voluntary prepayments across 352K eligible observations, the event rate is low (0.26% SMM). The model achieves AUC 0.72 on out-of-time test data, indicating meaningful predictive power but room for improvement with more history or exogenous rate data.
